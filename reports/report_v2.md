# LLM Creative Writing Analysis — v2 Metrics Report

_Companion to [report.md](report.md). Status: draft · 2026-06-19_

## 1. Introduction

The original study ([report.md](report.md)) measured **cross-run repetition and
consistency** — text/semantic similarity, named-entity and name-component reuse,
vocabulary diversity, and structure — to surface model "fingerprints." Three gaps
were visible from that work:

1. **The sound/shape of names was invisible.** The V-surname signature (report
   §6.6) recurs *phonetically* across vendors even where exact tokens never repeat;
   exact-string matching cannot see it.
2. **No per-text craft signal.** v1 measured how *varied* the N outputs were, never
   any property of a single piece (rhythm, dialogue balance, cliché, internal
   repetition).
3. **The diversity layer stopped at exact and semantic.** Nothing sat between
   verbatim overlap and meaning — the n-gram-surface layer the NLG literature uses.

This report presents the first results from the **v2 metric library** (`utils/metrics/`),
eight opt-in metrics designed to close those gaps. They are computed *without
altering the v1 results*: each `results_*.json` gets a parallel
`results_*.metrics.json` sidecar, so the frozen 2025/2026 corpora are re-scored,
not regenerated, and the original artifacts stay byte-identical.

The eight metrics:

| Metric | Layer | Measures |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| `phonetic_names` | diversity | character-name recurrence by leading **sound**, not token |
| `ngram_diversity` | diversity | corpus-level **distinct-n** + **Self-BLEU** across the N runs |
| `mtld` | diversity | **length-robust** lexical diversity (vs length-sensitive TTR) |
| `opening_lines` | diversity | first-sentence similarity (lexical + semantic) |
| `burstiness` | craft | sentence-length variation (rhythm) |
| `dialogue_ratio` | craft | share of text inside quotation marks |
| `intra_text_repetition` | craft | word/phrase overuse *within* one story |
| `cliche_density` | craft | cliché / stock-phrase + em-dash density |

## 2. Methodology

### 2.1 Corpus

The v2 metrics were run over the same frozen corpus as the v1 longitudinal study:
**13 models, 10 generation runs each (140 runs total)**, split into two cohorts:

- **2025 API cohort (7 models):** GPT-4o, o1, o3, Gemini 2.0 Pro, Gemini 2.5 Pro,
  Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Claude 3.7 Sonnet — generated via provider SDKs at a fixed
  sampling temperature.
- **2026 CLI cohort (6 models):** codex-cli, gemini-cli-flash, gemini-cli-pro,
  claude-cli-opus, claude-cli-sonnet, claude-cli-fable — generated by shelling out
  to local agent CLIs in headless mode.

### 2.2 Metric definitions (brief)

- **MTLD** — Measure of Textual Lexical Diversity (McCarthy & Jarvis 2010),
  bidirectional, threshold 0.72. Higher = richer vocabulary; unlike TTR it does not
  fall merely because a text is longer.
- **distinct-n / Self-BLEU** — over the *set* of 10 runs. distinct-n = distinct/total
  n-grams (higher = more diverse); Self-BLEU = each run scored against the other
  nine (higher = runs more **alike**). They move in opposite directions.
- **opening_lines** — first sentence of each run (after stripping `Title:`/heading
  scaffolding), compared pairwise by Jaccard, difflib sequence ratio, and embedding
  cosine (`all-MiniLM-L6-v2`, the same model as v1 semantic similarity), plus the
  most common opening prefix.
- **burstiness** — per-run coefficient of variation (CV = std/mean) of sentence
  length, and the Goh–Barabási B = (std−mean)/(std+mean) ∈ [−1, 1] (>0 bursty/
  human-like, <0 metronomic). Higher CV = more varied rhythm.
- **dialogue_ratio** — words inside paired double quotes / total words.
- **intra_text_repetition** — per-text n-gram repeat rate (1 − distinct/total);
  unigram over content words only.
- **cliche_density** — per-1000-word hits for a curated cliché-phrase lexicon
  (frozen "lexicon v1"), a separate single-word "slop" list, and em-dash density.
- **phonetic_names** — character surnames clustered by leading sound; reports the
  maximum recurrence of any sound across the 10 runs.

### 2.3 Caveats (read before trusting any single number)

- **CLI-vs-API sampling confound.** The CLI backends do not forward sampling
  parameters and use each CLI's own defaults; the 2026 cohort is therefore **not
  strictly comparable** to the 2025 API cohort on any axis sensitive to sampling.
  (Even within the API cohort, Fable/Opus-class models that drop `temperature`
  differ from the temp-0.7 runs.) Cross-cohort comparisons below are directional.
- **Length sensitivity.** `intra_text_repetition` and `distinct-n` both drift with
  document length (longer texts mechanically repeat more / dilute distinct ratios).
  MTLD, burstiness CV, and the per-1000-word cliché rates are the length-robust
  axes; treat intra-repetition as length-confounded (note the longest models, o1
  and codex-cli, top it).
- **NER false positives.** `phonetic_names` inherits spaCy `PERSON` mislabels and,
  because common consonants recur, its *raw maximum* saturates near 10/10 for most
  models — it is not discriminating on its own. The signal of interest is the
  *specific* cross-vendor cluster (the V-surnames of report §6.6), not the max.
- **Lexicon recall.** `cliche_density`'s lexicon v1 was tuned on this science-fiction
  corpus; it validates curated candidates rather than discovering new clichés, so it
  under-counts genres it wasn't tuned on. It is a *generic-prose* signal, not an
  AI-authorship detector.

## 4. Findings

### 4.1 GPT-4o is the generic-prose outlier

GPT-4o tops both slop axes by a wide margin — cliché density **2.83/1k** (~4× the
next model, o1 at 0.67) and slop-word density **6.17/1k** (>3× the next) — while
also having the **flattest rhythm** (CV 0.47, B −0.37) and being the **most
repetitive across its own runs** (Self-BLEU 0.218, near the top). It does this in
the **shortest** outputs (1055 words). Taken together the v2 metrics paint a
consistent picture of comparatively formulaic, low-variation prose — the profile
the "AI-slop" critique points at.

### 4.2 o3 is the high-craft outlier

o3 sits at the opposite pole on the diversity axes: the **highest MTLD** (327, vs a
~200–220 cluster for the other API models), the **most cross-run variety**
(Self-BLEU 0.054 and distinct-2 0.83, both best in class), **zero** cliché-phrase
hits, and maximally **varied openings** (1/10 shared prefix). Its one strong habit
is punctuation (see §4.4).

### 4.3 Claude 3.5 Sonnet's formulaic-opening tell

Opening-line analysis isolates a tell that whole-text similarity dilutes:
**8 of 10** Claude 3.5 Sonnet runs open with the same words (in this corpus, "The
harvest drones…"), and its opening sentences are the most semantically alike of any
model (mean cosine **0.582**). This cross-validates v1, where Claude 3.5 Sonnet also
had the **highest** whole-text semantic similarity (0.648, report §3.1) — the new
metric localizes that convergence to the opening. No other model exceeds 4/10 on the
shared-opening prefix except gemini-cli-flash (7/10).

### 4.4 Em-dash density is a clean vendor signature

The single sharpest separator in the dataset is em-dash use. The **Claude family
plus o3** are heavy (12.2–14.1 per 1000 words) while **Gemini and codex** are
essentially bare (0.0–0.23) — a ~175× spread between extremes. Crucially, the split
**persists across both the API and CLI paths** (API Claude 3.7 at 12.2; CLI
claude-cli-* at 12.9–14.1; codex-cli at 0.08; API Gemini 2.0 at 0.0), which makes it
look like a durable house-style trait rather than a sampling artifact.

### 4.5 Dialogue balance and rhythm

The Claude API models are the most **dialogue-forward** (Claude 3.7 at 26%, 3.5 at
19%), while Gemini 2.0 is almost pure narration (3%). On rhythm, the CLI Claude
models show the most sentence-length variation (CV 0.89–0.98) and GPT-4o the least
(0.47) — but because rhythm is one of the axes least insulated from the CLI/API
sampling difference, the CLI-vs-API contrast here should be read with §2.3 in mind.

### 4.6 Cohort-level pattern

Within the length-robust axes, the 2026 CLI cohort clusters **lower on MTLD**
(93–162) than the 2025 API cohort (110–327), with codex-cli the most diverse CLI
model. We do **not** attribute this to model capability: the sampling confound
(§2.3) is the more parsimonious explanation, and removing it requires the
API-at-fixed-temperature re-run already noted as future work in the v1 report.

### 4.7 Craft-proxy scorecard (which model "leads"?)

The metrics do **not** measure quality directly, so there is no principled single
"best." As a rough synthesis only, we rank all 13 models on six craft-relevant axes
and count how many each lands in the **top-3** vs **bottom-3**:

Read this as a **heuristic, not a ranking**: (1) it sums axes that measure different
things; (2) the axes are *variety/craft proxies*, not quality — a model can score
well by being merely erratic; (3) it mixes the API and CLI cohorts, which differ by
the sampling confound (§2.3), so o3 (API) and the Anthropic CLI tier are not
head-to-head; (4) two axes (cross-run variety, opening variety) reward generating
*different* stories from one prompt, which is desirable for exploration but is not
"is a single story good." The directionally robust statements are the ends:
**o3 is consistently strong**, the **Anthropic CLI tier looks strong**, **Fable 5
owns rhythm specifically**, and **GPT-4o is consistently weak** — but a genuine
quality verdict awaits the Phase-4 LLM-judge.

## 5. Limitations

- All §4 cross-cohort statements inherit the **CLI-vs-API sampling confound**; they
  are descriptive of *these* runs, not controlled comparisons.
- `intra_text_repetition` and `distinct-n` are **length-correlated**; the longest
  models (o1, codex-cli) lead intra-repetition partly for being long.
- `phonetic_names`' raw maximum is **not discriminating** (common sounds saturate)
  and inherits **NER false positives**; the V-surname cluster (report §6.6) remains
  the metric's real payload and is best inspected per-cluster, not via the max.
- `cliche_density` reflects a **curated, sci-fi-tuned lexicon v1** and is a
  generic-prose proxy, not an authorship test.
- There is still **no quality ground-truth**: these are automated proxies. The
  planned LLM-as-judge rubric (Phase 4) is the intended anchor.

## 6. Conclusions and next steps

The v2 metrics reproduce and sharpen the v1 picture while adding genuinely new
signal. Two model-level fingerprints stand out as robust: **GPT-4o's generic-prose
profile** (high cliché/slop, flat rhythm, high cross-run repetition) and the
**em-dash vendor signature** (Claude + o3 heavy, Gemini + codex bare) that survives
the API→CLI transition. Opening-line analysis localized **Claude 3.5 Sonnet's**
known thematic convergence to a concrete, eyeball-able tell (8/10 identical
openings), and the diversity axes cleanly rank **o3** as the most varied generator
in the set.

Recommended next work, in roadmap order:

1. **API-at-fixed-temperature re-run** to dissolve the CLI/API confound and make the
   cohort comparisons controlled.
2. **Formalize the NER-aware name metric** (stop-list + hyphen handling) so the
   phonetic/name signals are trustworthy without manual cross-checking.
3. **Phase 4 LLM-as-judge rubric** to add the quality ground-truth these automated
   proxies cannot provide, with the usual bias controls (no self-judging, randomized
   pairwise order, recorded judge identity).
